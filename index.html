Mexican Candles

History

Mexican candles, or "veladoras," are a colorful and vibrant part of Mexican culture and tradition. They are often used in religious ceremonies, such as Day of the Dead celebrations, where families honor their deceased loved ones. These candles come in various shapes, sizes, and colors, each carrying its own symbolic meaning.

Traditionally, these candles were usually made by women at home and each would be as tall as four feet and would take a whole day from start to finish. They often feature fragrances like cinnamon, coconut, or vanilla, adding to the sensory experience.
